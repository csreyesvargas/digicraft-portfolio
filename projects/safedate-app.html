Project Overview

The Problem

Meeting strangers from dating apps or online platforms carries real safety risks — especially for users with no way to verify who they're actually meeting. SafeDate was designed to fill that gap: a mobile app that lets users verify people's identities before a meetup, access emergency SOS features in a crisis, and build confidence in their personal safety.

My Role

I led the full UX design process — from defining user flows and information architecture to designing high-fidelity screens and building an interactive prototype in Figma. I also documented safety-focused interaction design decisions and identified areas for accessibility improvement.

Reflection

What I Learned

This project challenged me to think deeply about safety-critical UX — where unclear labels or missed interaction states could genuinely affect someone in a dangerous situation. It reinforced that accessibility isn't optional: button sizes, tap areas, and screen hierarchy matter especially when a user is stressed.

If I continued iterating, I'd run usability tests focused on the SOS flow specifically, and explore how to make the premium upsell feel helpful rather than intrusive.
